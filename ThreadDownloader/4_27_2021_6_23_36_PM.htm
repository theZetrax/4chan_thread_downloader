Although appealing on a base level, Utilitarianism is impractical and potentially harmful. A moral agent operating under Utilitarianism is effectively guessing the consequences of their actions (that is to say consequences are difficult to predict), nor can a moral agent easily qualify what pleasure is. You can point to the Hedonic Calculus as providing a guide that remedies these aforementioned problems, but you will find that in practice the Hedonic Calculus cannot realistically be used day-to-day. Furthermore, the manner in which Utilitarianism focuses on the greater good is also dangerous in that it: 1) allows a majority to run roughshod over a minority, and 2) can see the interests and integrity of a moral agent eliminated. Finally, (although I suppose this will depend one's stance) the fundamental issue is that pleasure and consequences are not the only things relevant when it comes to ethics and morality.
